I.Introduction

A.What is DNA Portrait Landscape?

Predicting human morphological traits using genomic data (aka, DNA portraying or DNA phenotyping) shows promising prospect in forensic studies. To fill up the vacancy of single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) data related to human morphological characters and to make SNP data more friendly to use, we establish a DNA portrait landscape, short DNAportrait, which is the first portrait-specific database focusing on the relationship between genes and different kinds of morphological characteristics of humans. DNAportrait is a web-based, open-access resource comprising over 500 genes that effect on human externally visual characteristics such as eye color, skin color, hair curling etc. In addition, DNAportrait integrated data through hundreds of articles manually, which provides information including gene name, appearance trait, SNP position, variation annotation, effect value and so on. All the data makes DNAportrait the first comprehensive resource for forensic studies on genomic data and morphologies.

II.Datasets and Curation Model

A.Datasets

The data is collected through a manually curation workflow. Relevant publication are obtained by extensive search of peer-reviewed articles from PubMed (PubMed: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/) and the Web of Science database (Web of Science: https://apps.webofknowledge.com/) using keywords with combinations of: (i) genetic facial trait; (ii) genetic hair trait; (iii) genetic eye color or iris color trait; (iv) genetic skin trait. Totally, we deposited more than 1183items, including 344 non-redundant SNPs, 510differentgenes.The number of genes involved in hair, skin, eye, and facial characters are 127, 207, 32 and 129 respectively. The detailed numbers of genes related to sub-traits are shown in Statistics page.

B.Curation model

III.Database Usage

A.What information is provided for DNA Portrait Landscape?

Information in DNAportraitsuch as SNP rs-ids, attribute of variant (reference allele, alternative allele, MAF), nearby genes of the variant, the related morphological traits, the effect size of the variant in the original study and the p-value of the statistical test,the corresponding study population and provenance reference (PubMed ID, year of publication, title of paper), etc. areretrieved.

B. How can I get detailed information for any human morphological trait of interest?

If you have some interest gene or rsID or chromosome or trait, please visit the page of Home, and you can search the information in the search box. Or you can visit the page of Traits, SNPS, Populations, and filter some conditions, and it will display the query results you interested in different kinds of details.
